1. The Injection Moulding Bottleneck

An imaginary phone manufacturer (phonesRus) has approached us to produce the top and bottom casings for their mobile devices. Typically the main manufacturing method for the production of mobile phone casings is injection moulding. Adhesives Technologies (Henkel) developments offer some insight into what can happen when injection moulding goes wrong. To achieve strong bonding properties, highly reactive additives are required which, in this case, became overly corrosive when heated. The additives began ‘consuming’ the tooling effectively altering the precision of the mould resulting in incompatible components. Turn around for rebuilt tools resulted in a delay where a 12 week lead time became a 24 week delay.

Incorporating industry 4.0 technologies such as additive manufacturing and digital twins can help to stretch the possibilities rendered impossible by the rigidity of injection moulding (Lasi et al., 2014). Merit3D used photopolymer carbon DLS to rapidly prototype iterations in a single day. Within three days the hangers were on the production line using high speed printing technology to print thousands of parts per day. Merit3D proved that additive manufacturing can replace traditional manufacturing methods such as injection moulding and as such, it will be the method of manufacture that we will utilise in this undertaking.

Slicing Engine Optimization: Arachne

One of the biggest technical leaps for the snap-fits was switching to the Arachne wall engine. Traditional slicing engines often struggled with the tiny, precise geometry of the casing clips, either over-extruding or creating weak "thin-walls." By using Arachne, the slicer could variably adjust the line width, making the clips much more resilient to the physical stress of the Festo press station.

7. Digital Twin Validation & Reflection

Critical Analysis of Resource Waste

Looking at the 80% worker idle time, a traditional Lean approach might see this as Muda (waste). However, in Industry 4.0, this "Idle" time is actually agility. This worker is essentially a high-level supervisor, available for intervention or fleet maintenance without stopping the 6-minute tactical takt. This shift from labor to oversight is the hallmark of the 4th industrial revolution.

Sustainability & Resillience

By using additive manufacturing, we reduced the £100,000 sunk cost of traditional tooling. More importantly, the ability to tweak bridging settings rather than rebuilding a steel mould significantly reduces material waste. This Digital Twin isn't just about speed; it's about building a more sustainable manufacturing backbone.
